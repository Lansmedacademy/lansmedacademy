# Type 1 vs Type 2 Diabetes: What's the Difference?

Many people think all diabetes is the same, but **Type 1 and Type 2 Diabetes are completely different diseases**. They both cause high blood sugar, but they happen for different reasons.

## What is Type 1 Diabetes?

Type 1 Diabetes happens when the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells (β-cells) in the pancreas.

As a result, the pancreas produces **little or no insulin**.

Without insulin, glucose cannot enter the body's cells to produce energy, so sugar accumulates in the bloodstream.

Type 1 Diabetes usually develops in **children, teenagers, and young adults**, although it can occur at any age.

People with Type 1 Diabetes need **lifelong insulin injections** because their bodies cannot make enough insulin to survive.

### Key points about Type 1 Diabetes:

• Autoimmune disease
• Little or no insulin production
• Usually affects young people
• Sudden onset of symptoms
• Requires lifelong insulin treatment
• Higher risk of Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

## What is Type 2 Diabetes?

Type 2 Diabetes occurs when the body's cells become **resistant to insulin**.

In the beginning, the pancreas still produces insulin, sometimes even more than normal. However, the body's cells do not respond properly to it.

Over time, the pancreas becomes exhausted and cannot produce enough insulin, causing blood sugar levels to rise.

Type 2 Diabetes is more common in **adults and people who are overweight or obese**, although it is increasingly being seen in younger people because of unhealthy lifestyles.

Many people with Type 2 Diabetes can initially control their condition with **diet, exercise, and medications**, but some may eventually need insulin.

### Key points about Type 2 Diabetes:

• Caused by insulin resistance
• Insulin is present but does not work effectively
• Usually associated with obesity and lifestyle factors
• Develops gradually over many years
• Often treated with lifestyle changes and medications
• Higher risk of Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)

## Symptoms of Both Types of Diabetes

Both Type 1 and Type 2 Diabetes can cause:

• Frequent urination (Polyuria)
• Excessive thirst (Polydipsia)
• Increased hunger (Polyphagia)
• Fatigue and weakness
• Blurred vision
• Weight changes

People with Type 2 Diabetes may also experience:

• Slow wound healing
• Recurrent infections
• Numbness or tingling in the hands and feet

## The Simple Difference

**Type 1 Diabetes:** The pancreas is damaged and cannot make enough insulin.

**Type 2 Diabetes:** The pancreas still makes insulin, but the body's cells no longer respond to it properly.

### Easy way to remember:

**Type 1 = No insulin.**

**Type 2 = Insulin doesn't work properly.**

Understanding this difference is important because early diagnosis and proper treatment can prevent serious complications such as blindness, kidney failure, heart disease, and stroke.
